Rostering & Account Setup

What changed in the new system and how to run your workflows.

Part 1

Rostering & Connections

The Rostering tab lives on the Settings page (the top, all-districts layer) and shows every district's status at a glance. Click into a district to open its Connections tab, which lives inside that district and holds the connection, its sync history and change log, the course selection — where you choose which of that district's courses to sync — and a rostering problems panel that flags anything a sync couldn't complete cleanly.

On the Rostering tab (Settings page):

  • District status — a quick read on every district's rostering health.

Inside a district, the Connections tab shows:

  • Connection — the roster provider (Clever or ClassLink, for now) and whether it's connected.
  • Last sync summary — a snapshot of the most recent sync.
  • Synced courses — choose which of the district's courses sync in. This is your first filter (see Part 5).
  • Rostering problems — anything a sync couldn't complete cleanly. Some are engineering's to fix (a failed or stuck sync, bad data from the provider) — just flag the district to the engineering team. Others are yours (a course not synced, a group with no active paid combo, a missing co-teacher) — resolve those in the UI and re-sync.
  • Sync history — each sync's time, trigger, and result.
  • Change log — what each sync changed, record by record.

Part 3

Group names & finding the right group

Naming standard: group names are automatically prefixed with the primary teacher and co-teacher last names in parentheses, so you can tell who a group belongs to without opening it.

Where the names come from: the prefix is built from the group's primary teacher and co-teacher slots. If neither slot is filled, it falls back to the first faculty member in the list instead.

Group picker: when you choose a group, the picker now shows the school and district names. Across an account with more than one school or district, you can see exactly which group you're selecting.

Part 4

Active vs. Inactive

The active/inactive flag now decides whether a record counts — not whether it exists. Since everything comes in from rostering, flipping records is a core part of the job, and we've made it easy.

  • Works across all layers: district, school, group, faculty, student.
  • Deactivating cascades down. Turn off a district and everything inside it — schools, groups, faculty, students — goes inactive too. Same for a school: its groups and people follow.
  • Status column — every layer's table has a Status column, so you can read each record's active/inactive state at a glance.
  • Filter by status — an All / Active / Inactive toggle on each layer lets you show everything or narrow to one; inactive isn't hidden.
  • Bulk apply active/inactive on any layer — select records, then Activate or Deactivate.
  • This is how you curate: deactivate groups, faculty or students you shouldn't be using; the record stays for reference but stops counting.
Heads up: because deactivation cascades, switching off a district or school takes everything beneath it with it. Deactivate at the layer you actually mean.

Year rollover

At the start of each new school year this happens automatically — you don't run it. CS gets an email reminder two weeks before.

  • The new roster comes in and creates the new year's groups, associating the relevant students and faculty.
  • Last year's groups are retired: a group with no students and no product combos is deleted; every other group is set to Inactive, keeping its combo and roster for historical reporting and renewals.

Net effect: the old group goes inactive and the new one comes to life. From there, treat the new groups exactly like Part 5 — confirm them, add the paid product combo, set Active.

Part 5

Activating students & faculty

The roster used to decide who's in. Now an active group with a paid product combo decides.

Everything on the roster syncs in — people and their groups. That's raw data; it doesn't activate anyone on its own. A student goes live only when they're in an active group with a paid product combo — the bundle of products the school actually paid for.

The job, in three moves

  1. Sync the right courses in the district's Connections tab.
  2. Review the groups, add a paid product combo, set them Active.
  3. Deactivate anything that doesn't apply.
  1. Go to the district's Connections tab, select courses, and sync.

    In the district's Connections tab, under Synced courses (Part 1), pick which of the district's courses to sync, then run the sync. Everyone in those courses comes in — people and their groups — as raw data, not active yet. Leave a course unselected (a math-only course, say) and those students never enter the system.

  2. Review the groups, activate them, and add products.

    Groups come in from rostering — you don't build them. Confirm which ones are correct, add the paid product combo, and set them Active. That's the gate: active group + paid product combo = the students go live. Apply a combo to one group, or bulk-apply across many at once.

    3. ScreenshotGroup list
    Merge vs. Replace. When bulk-applying combos, Merge adds the selected combos on top of what each group already has; Replace swaps their existing combos for the new selection. The dialog shows how many groups are in scope before you apply.

    Example combos (the full set is in the dialog): Avanza Reading with Phonics; Boost Reading with Phonics, Writing, Oral (ChatterCity); First Step + Online Library (Simplified / Traditional); Level Learning Reading with First Steps, Oral (ChatterCity), and Writing (Traditional).

    ScreenshotGroup config — paid product combo field + Active toggle
  3. Deactivate everything that doesn't apply.

    Faculty, students, or groups you shouldn't be using — deactivate them, one at a time or bulk on any layer (district, school, group, student). An inactive record stays for reference but stops counting. (See Part 4.)

Rule of thumb: no active group + paid product = the student doesn't count, even if they're in the roster.

Part 6

Districts, schools & admins — now self-service

These used to need a back-end request to engineering. Now you do them yourself, right in the UI:

  • Create / edit a district
  • Create / edit a school
  • Assign district admins and school admins

To create: use the create button at the top of the Schools or Districts list (for example, Add School) and fill out the form. To edit: click the pencil icon next to the school or district's name.

ScreenshotAssigning a district / school admin

How admin access works

You no longer add an admin to each group one by one. Granting someone district admin or school admin access automatically gives them the whole layer — they can see every group and all the data in that district or school.

This also happens on its own from rostering: faculty brought in through Clever or ClassLink who are marked as an admin automatically get access at their expected layer (school or district) — no manual step needed.

Words you'll see

Roster
The list of districts, schools, teachers, and students that the customer's system sends us.

When that roster data is pulled into our system and refreshed.

The services districts use to send us their roster.

The bundle of products a school paid for — for example, "Avanza Reading with Phonics."

Whether a record counts in the product right now. Inactive stays visible for history but doesn't count.
Layer
A level in the account — district, school, group, or student.
Feature flag
A switch that turns a specific feature on or off for one district.
